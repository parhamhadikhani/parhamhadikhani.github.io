About Me

I am a computer science Ph.D. student researching computer vision. I have been working on various domains such as routing protocols in wireless sensor networks, analyzing social media, applying unsupervised methods in computer vision, and application of deep learning in cheminformatics with the specialization in unsupervised deep learning, artificial and evolutionary algorithms. currently, I am working on 3 projects simultaneously including unsupervised human activity recognition, unsupervised deep clustering for image clustering, and application of deep learning in scaffold decoration for drug discovery. Upon completing my degree, I plan to continue teaching and researching Artificial Intelligence as a professor of computer science. My additional expertise and teaching interests are object-oriented programmings, software engineering, and Database Systems.
